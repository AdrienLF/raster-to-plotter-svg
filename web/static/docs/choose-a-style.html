Choose by intention, not by name

The right module is a bargain between the image, the physical pen, and the time you are willing to plot. Start with the mark you want, then tune density to what the pen can resolve.

Choose the vocabulary

Dots

Stippling and Random Stipple are neutral, tonal and forgiving. Ideal when the image should dominate the style.

Short marks

Dashes and Shapes reveal the sampling rhythm. Rotation fields can make the marks describe form.

Networks

Triangulation, Tree and Diagram turn sites into crystalline, branching or cracked structures.

Continuous paths

TSP, Spiral, Hatch and Streamlines reduce pen lifts and make the drawing process part of the image.

Tiles

Mosaic, Tessellations and Shape Dither trade photographic smoothness for a deliberate graphic system.

Growth

Differential Growth and Circle Packing create organisms rather than screens; allow more iteration time.

Pen width sets the real resolution

Pen width is not cosmetic. It tells PlotterForge how much detail the physical tool can resolve. Set the measured width first, then judge density at Fit Page zoom.

  • 0.1–0.3 mm fineliner: detailed stippling, engraving, diagrams and fine tessellations are realistic.
  • 0.4–0.8 mm technical pen: reduce point counts; prefer hatch, dashes, larger shapes and open streamlines.
  • 1 mm and above: treat the mark as a graphic unit. Shape Dither, broad hatch, generators and sparse packing remain legible.
  • Flat nib: preview the nib angle, but remember the SVG remains a centreline—the physical nib makes the width.

Generation cost and plotting cost are different. TSP can be expensive to compute but economical to plot; thousands of independent dots compute quickly yet spend real time on pen lifts.

Starting recipes

Soft portrait, 0.3 mm black pen

Voronoi Stippling, Point Density 700, Ignore White on, Stipple Size 0.75. Raise Contrast only after the face reads at Fit Page zoom.

Graphic portrait, three pens

Shape Dither with 32–45 columns, 4–6 tone levels, error diffusion on, and a simple uploaded symbol. Use Luminance distribution for a tonal pen set.

One-line landscape

Adaptive TSP for the first draft, then Voronoi TSP for a more settled final route. Cap points before increasing quality.

Fabric, fur or wind

Engraving or Streamlines Edge Field. Bind direction to a mix of edge strength and a painted field; keep density tied to image tone.
